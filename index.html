SAHARA

The Sahara Desert is the largest hot desert in the world, stretching across much of North Africa. Covering an area of about 9.2 million square kilometers (3.6 million square miles), it is nearly the size of the United States or China. The Sahara spans 11 countries, including Algeria, Chad, Egypt, Libya, Mali, Mauritania, Morocco, Niger, Sudan, Tunisia, and Western Sahara.
